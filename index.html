Quick-Start Prompting Guide

Task

Clearly define what you want the AI to accomplish

Persona

Specify the role or expertise level the AI should adopt

Content/Context

Provide relevant background information and constraints

Format

Specify the desired structure and presentation style

Output Requirements

Define success criteria and specific deliverables

Prompting Paradigms Overview

Zero-Shot

Ask the AI to perform a task without providing examples. Best for straightforward, well-defined requests.

One-Shot

Provide exactly one example to guide the AI's response format and style.

Few-Shot

Give multiple examples to establish patterns and improve accuracy for complex tasks.

Chain-of-Thought

Encourage step-by-step reasoning by asking the AI to "think through" the problem aloud.

Tree-of-Thought

Explore multiple reasoning paths simultaneously, useful for complex problem-solving scenarios.

Role-Prompting

Assign specific expertise or perspective to the AI to improve response quality and relevance.

"Genius Mode" Power Phrases for Enhanced Results

1 Clarity-Seeking Phrase

Design a one-week crash-course syllabus that equips high-school teachers to use generative-AI tools in formative assessment; ask me all the questions until you're 95% confident you can complete the task successfully.

Use this to ensure the AI gathers all necessary information before proceeding.

2 Expert-Level Thinking

List the three most overlooked ethical risks of deploying AI chatbots in college admissions offices; what would the top 0.1% experts in this field think?

Elevates the AI's reasoning to expert-level insights and considerations.

3 Perspective-Reframing

Draft a one-page concept note for a mobile AI-literacy lab that serves rural Texas schools; reframe this in a way that challenges how I see the problem.

Challenges assumptions and provides fresh perspectives on familiar problems.

Real-World Prompt Examples Library

Multilingual Content Creation

Purpose: Instantly translate and adapt literacy materials for diverse populations.

Create a Spanish version of this adult education worksheet, maintaining reading level and cultural relevance for Texas Hispanic communities. Include pronunciation guides for key vocabulary.

Impact: Serve 39% of Texas population more effectively, reduce translation costs by 80%

Emergency Communication

Scenario: Hurricane approaching, need to quickly inform Spanish-speaking families about class cancellations.

Translate this emergency notice into Spanish for adult education families in Texas. Keep language simple (5th grade level) and include action steps: "Classes are cancelled Tuesday-Thursday due to Hurricane conditions. Safety first! Check our website Friday morning for updates. Free childcare will be available when we reopen. Questions? Call Maria at 555-0123."

Personalized Learning Materials

Purpose: Create custom reading passages at specific grade/literacy levels.

Write a 200-word passage about job interview skills for adult learners reading at a 6th-grade level. Include vocabulary words: professional, qualifications, references, punctual. Make it relevant to Texas job market.

Impact: Reduce material prep time by 75%, increase learner engagement through relevance

Lesson Plan Adaptation

Scenario: Need to modify existing curriculum for learners with different backgrounds.

Adapt this financial literacy lesson about budgeting for adults who primarily work in agricultural/seasonal employment in Texas. Original lesson assumes steady monthly income. Keep same learning objectives but make examples relevant to fluctuating seasonal wages.

Grant Writing Assistant

Purpose: Streamline proposal development and improve funding success rates.

Based on this RFP from Texas Education Agency and our program data, draft a compelling needs statement for our adult basic education program. Emphasize our impact on workforce development in rural Texas counties.

Impact: Increase grant success rate by 30%, save 40 hours per proposal

Family Engagement

Scenario: Creating materials to help parents support children's reading at home.

Create a one-page guide for parents with limited English proficiency on how to support their child's reading at home. Include 5 specific activities that don't require perfect English, use materials found in most homes, and connect to Texas cultural values around family education.
